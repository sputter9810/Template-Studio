# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound warm, professional, reassuring while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Warm
- Professional
- Reassuring

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Gentle but clear. Focus on how clients feel after class.

## CTA Style
Book a Class, View Timetable, Start with an Intro Pack.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Invite beginners in with calm, confidence-building language and a clear first step.

### About
Focus on teacher quality, studio atmosphere, and benefits of consistent practice.

### Services / Products / Menu
Differentiate reformer, mat, private sessions, and beginner pathways.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Highlight posture, energy, confidence, and supportive teaching.

### Contact
Make location, parking, beginner info, and booking process easy to find.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use buyer-friendly, premium placeholder copy with realistic studio language.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 5–9 words
- Hero supporting text: 20–36 words
- Section intro: 18–32 words
- Card text: 14–28 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- strengthen
- lengthen
- restore
- guided
- studio

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- bootcamp
- grind
- extreme
- aggressive

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
