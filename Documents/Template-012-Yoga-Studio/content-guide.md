# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The website should sound warm, supportive, beginner-friendly while guiding visitors toward the main action: book an intro class.

## Tone of Voice
- Clear
- Trustworthy
- Conversion-focused

## Writing Style Rules
- Lead with outcomes and relevance to the target buyer
- Keep paragraphs short and scannable
- Use straightforward, non-technical language
- Repeat the main CTA naturally throughout the page

## Headline Style
Headlines should be specific, benefit-led, and tailored to the niche rather than sounding generic.

## CTA Style
Use direct, action-based CTAs such as “Start your first class” or close equivalents that combine action and outcome.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
State the offer clearly, communicate the core benefit quickly, and place the primary CTA immediately.

### About
Focus on why the business is credible, different, and relevant to the customer.

### Services / Products / Menu
Prioritise the most saleable offerings first and keep descriptions concise and buyer-friendly.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use believable, benefit-led testimonials and support them with role, result, or context where appropriate.

### Contact
Keep contact copy simple, reassuring, and action-oriented. Reduce friction and remind the visitor what happens next.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic starter copy that a buyer can customise quickly. Avoid lorem ipsum and avoid making the template feel unfinished.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 5–10 words
- Hero supporting text: 1–3 short sentences
- Section intro: 1 short paragraph
- Card text: 1–2 concise sentences

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- Move, breathe, reset
- Feel welcome from day one
- Find your practice

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- Aggressive sales tone
- Hardcore fitness jargon

## Notes
The copy should support conversion before explanation. It should help buyers imagine a real business using the template.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
