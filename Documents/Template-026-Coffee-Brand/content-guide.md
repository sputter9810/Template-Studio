# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound craft-focused, warm, confident while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Craft-focused
- Warm
- Confident

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Sensory and brand-led, but still clear on action.

## CTA Style
Shop Coffee, Explore the Range, Enquire Wholesale.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Lead with freshness, flavour, and why this roast matters.

### About
Tell the roasting story, sourcing approach, and what sets the brand apart.

### Services / Products / Menu
Treat product blocks like signature offerings with tasting note cues and simple buying paths.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use proof around taste, consistency, and service.

### Contact
Clarify retail hours, café location, and wholesale contact paths.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic tasting-note and product-style placeholder copy.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 4–8 words
- Hero supporting text: 18–32 words
- Section intro: 16–30 words
- Card text: 10–22 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- roasted fresh
- origin
- blend
- single origin
- subscription

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- pretentious jargon overload
- generic café clichés
- overly corporate language

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
