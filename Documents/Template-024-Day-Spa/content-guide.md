# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound elegant, soothing, trustworthy while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Elegant
- Soothing
- Trustworthy

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Aspirational, sensory, short, and composed.

## CTA Style
Book Your Ritual, Explore Packages, Reserve Your Visit.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Sell the feeling of stepping out restored, not just the treatment menu.

### About
Describe the spa philosophy, service standard, and atmosphere.

### Services / Products / Menu
Organise treatments by experience or intention rather than long dense lists.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use emotional outcomes: relaxed, cared for, glowing, restored.

### Contact
Make booking, opening hours, arrival notes, and gift voucher questions easy.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use premium starter copy rather than lorem ipsum.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 5–9 words
- Hero supporting text: 20–40 words
- Section intro: 20–32 words
- Card text: 12–26 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- restore
- ritual
- unwind
- renew
- tailored care

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- cheap
- quick fix
- medicalised unless relevant
- hard sell

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
