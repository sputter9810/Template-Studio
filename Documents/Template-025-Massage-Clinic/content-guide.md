# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound clear, reassuring, professional while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Clear
- Reassuring
- Professional

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Outcome-led and reassuring, centred on relief, recovery, and care.

## CTA Style
Book an Appointment, View Treatments, Contact the Clinic.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
State who the clinic helps and what first-time clients can expect.

### About
Highlight practitioner background, treatment approach, and care standards.

### Services / Products / Menu
Make each treatment purpose obvious: pain relief, recovery, relaxation, mobility.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Show practical improvements and comfort with the practitioner.

### Contact
Include booking flow, availability, and what to bring or wear if helpful.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic clinic-style copy suitable for local service businesses.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 5–10 words
- Hero supporting text: 18–34 words
- Section intro: 18–28 words
- Card text: 12–24 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- relief
- recovery
- tailored treatment
- care
- practical support

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- cure-all language
- medical overclaiming
- mystical jargon unless brand-specific

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
