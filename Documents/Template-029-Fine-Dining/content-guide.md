# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound refined, confident, inviting while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Refined
- Confident
- Inviting

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Sparse, evocative, and premium.

## CTA Style
Reserve a Table, View the Experience, Enquire Privately.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Sell the evening, atmosphere, and anticipation, then make reservation action obvious.

### About
Tell the chef or venue philosophy with restraint.

### Services / Products / Menu
Use signature dishes or tasting-path language rather than exhaustive menu detail.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use short premium proof: memorable, polished, intimate, exceptional.

### Contact
Include reservation, hours, address, and private dining pathway clearly.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use premium starter copy suitable for upscale hospitality.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 3–8 words
- Hero supporting text: 18–34 words
- Section intro: 16–28 words
- Card text: 8–20 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- seasonal
- chef-led
- curated
- reservation
- experience

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- casual slang
- menu overload
- salesy discount language

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
