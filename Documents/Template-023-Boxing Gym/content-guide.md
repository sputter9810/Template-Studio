# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound direct, motivating, credible while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Direct
- Motivating
- Credible

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Short, forceful, and outcome-led.

## CTA Style
Start Training, Claim Your Trial, View Classes.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Communicate that beginners are welcome and every class has structure and coaching.

### About
Focus on gym culture, discipline options, and coach quality.

### Services / Products / Menu
Separate adult, youth, skills, striking, grappling, conditioning, and private training clearly.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Show progress in fitness, confidence, skill, and consistency.

### Contact
Give clear trial instructions, what to bring, and how to get in touch.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic gym copy rather than hype-only slogans.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 4–8 words
- Hero supporting text: 18–30 words
- Section intro: 16–28 words
- Card text: 10–24 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- confidence
- discipline
- technique
- conditioning
- community

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- violence
- intimidation
- fight club clichés
- ego-first language

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
