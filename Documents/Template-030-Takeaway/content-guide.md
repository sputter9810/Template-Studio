# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound fast, friendly, clear while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Fast
- Friendly
- Clear

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Immediate and action-led.

## CTA Style
Order Now, View the Menu, Get Delivery Info.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Lead with what they serve and make ordering the easiest next step on the page.

### About
Keep story short; focus on quality, speed, and customer favourites.

### Services / Products / Menu
Show bestsellers and bundles before the full list.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use short social proof with speed and taste cues.

### Contact
Prioritise address, ordering channels, and hours.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic menu-item starter copy with short, scan-friendly descriptions.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 3–7 words
- Hero supporting text: 12–24 words
- Section intro: 12–24 words
- Card text: 6–16 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- order fast
- pickup
- local favourite
- loaded
- fresh

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- long-form storytelling in hero
- formal dining language
- hidden order paths

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
