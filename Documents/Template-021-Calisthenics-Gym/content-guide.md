# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound confident, motivating, clear while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Confident
- Motivating
- Clear

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Short, punchy, result-oriented. Lead with the transformation or skill outcome.

## CTA Style
Use active phrases like Start Training, Book a Trial, View Programs.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Lead with the end state: stronger, more mobile, more capable. Show that every level is welcome.

### About
Emphasise coaching method, progression, safety, and community.

### Services / Products / Menu
Frame offers as pathways: foundations, strength, skills, open gym.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Highlight progress, confidence, pain-free training, and supportive culture.

### Contact
Reduce friction: tell people exactly how to start and what happens next.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic starter copy that a buyer can keep or lightly edit.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 6–10 words
- Hero supporting text: 18–32 words
- Section intro: 18–28 words
- Card text: 12–24 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- strength
- control
- progress
- coaching
- movement quality

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- hardcore only
- elite only
- shredded
- no pain no gain

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
