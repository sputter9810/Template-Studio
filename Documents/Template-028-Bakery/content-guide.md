# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound warm, appetising, local while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Warm
- Appetising
- Local

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Freshness-first and appetite-led.

## CTA Style
Visit Today, Ask About Orders, Explore the Range.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Create immediate sensory appeal and make visiting today feel easy.

### About
Focus on baking philosophy, ingredients, and neighbourhood identity.

### Services / Products / Menu
Use featured bakes, seasonal items, and order-friendly copy instead of giant menus.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
Use short proof that reinforces quality and local loyalty.

### Contact
Prioritise hours, location, and order/catering details.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use realistic bakery product names and simple starter descriptions.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 4–9 words
- Hero supporting text: 16–28 words
- Section intro: 16–28 words
- Card text: 8–18 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- baked fresh
- artisan
- seasonal
- small-batch
- daily favourites

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- cold corporate language
- too much jargon
- long dense menus

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
