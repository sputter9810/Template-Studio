# Content Guide

## Brand / Voice Summary
The site should sound playful, clear, inviting while staying clearly aligned to the buyer’s main action.

## Tone of Voice
- Playful
- Clear
- Inviting

## Writing Style Rules
- Write for scanning first, then detail second
- Lead with customer outcome before explaining features
- Keep paragraphs compact and practical
- Use realistic placeholder copy the buyer could actually keep

## Headline Style
Fast, flavour-first, and direct.

## CTA Style
Find Us This Week, View the Menu, Book Catering.

## Section Copy Guidance
### Hero
Immediately say what kind of food it is and where the visitor should go next.

### About
Show the truck’s personality and why it stands out.

### Services / Products / Menu
Focus on signature items and catering/event flexibility.

### Testimonials / Social Proof
If used, keep them short and energetic.

### Contact
Make the enquiry path dead simple: location, dates, and booking contact.

## Placeholder Content Rules
Use lively starter copy with realistic menu examples.

## Length Guidelines
- Hero headline: 3–8 words
- Hero supporting text: 14–26 words
- Section intro: 14–26 words
- Card text: 8–18 words

## Words / Phrases to Prefer
- fresh
- stacked
- loaded
- event-ready
- street food

## Words / Phrases to Avoid
- overly formal fine-dining copy
- generic placeholder names only
- vague scheduling

## Notes
Keep the copy specific enough to feel niche-relevant, but generic enough that a buyer can customise it quickly.

## Conversion Copy Rules
- Always lead with outcome, not feature
- Speak directly to the customer problem
- Use simple, non-technical language
- Avoid filler phrases

## CTA Strategy
- Primary CTA repeated every 1–2 sections
- Use action + benefit phrasing
- Avoid generic “Learn More”
